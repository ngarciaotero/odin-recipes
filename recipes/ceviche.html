Ceviche

Description

At its core, ceviche consists of raw fish cured in citrus juices, usually from lime or bitter orange. The fish is combined with onions, chili peppers, sweet potatoes, or cancha corn nuts, and served as a fresh, bright seafood cocktail. While ceviche's exact origins are debated, most food historians agree it has roots among the ancient Moche and Inca civilizations of Peru, where fish was cured in citrus due to lack of refrigeration. The dish became popularized when Spanish colonialists brought citrus fruits to the region during the 16th century.

Ingredients
  • shrimp
  • lime juice
  • cucumber
  • avocado
  • roma tomatoes
  • red onion
  • cilantro
  • cilantro
  • jalapeno
  • clamato juice
Steps
  1. Dice shrimp and place into a large glass (non-reactive) bowl. Squeeze 1 to 1 1/4 cups lime juice from 6 limes over shrimp. Stir to combine then cover and marinate in the refrigerator (marinate 1 1/2 to 2 hours for raw shrimp or until no longer translucent and marinate 15 minutes for cooked shrimp), stirring halfway through.
  2. Chop the vegetables: cucumber, avocado, tomatoes, red onion, jalapeno and cilantro. A food chopper will make this process much faster.
  3. When shrimp are done marinating, add vegetables to the bowl along with clamato juice and stir to combine. Serve with hot sauce if desired along with tortilla chips or over tostadas.

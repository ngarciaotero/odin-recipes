Tacos

Description

Tacos are an integral part of Mexican culinary heritage and considered one of Mexico's quintessential national dishes. Corn tortillas stuffed with a variety of fillings, tacos trace their origins to street food culture in Mexico, where they were an affordable and convenient food for workers. Traditional fillings include seasoned meat, chopped onion, cilantro, salsa, and lime juice. Tacos al pastor with spit-roasted pork is a popular modern iteration. From Tijuana to Mexico City and everywhere in between, tacos are a staple that brings families and friends together in taquerias and around street carts for a tasty, satisfying bite. They're also a symbol of Mexico's cultural identity, blending indigenous corn-based cuisine with Spanish-introduced ingredients like pork and beef.

Ingredients
  • vegetable oil
  • flank steak
  • onion
  • limes
  • cilantro
  • salt
  • pepper
  • butter
  • corn tortillas
Steps
  1. Thinly slice the steak. Then chop into bite site pieces.
  2. In a skillet, begin to brown the steak in the oil.
  3. Add half the onions to the skillet and cook until soft. Save the rest of the onions. Season with salt and pepper.
  4. Squeeze the juice of two fresh limes over the meat and onion mixture.
  5. While the steak is cooking, melt the butter in another skillet or I used an electric skillet.
  6. Fry the mini corn tortillas in the melted butter for 1 minute until they are soft.
  7. Top with cilantro, fresh onions and serve with limes.

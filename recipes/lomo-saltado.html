Lomo Saltado

Description

Lomo saltado is a Peruvian stir-fry dish that typically consists of thinly sliced beef, onions, tomatoes, french fries, and soy sauce. The dish originated in the 19th century as an adaptation of Chinese stir-fry techniques brought over by Chinese immigrants to Peru. The use of ingredients like tomato and potato demonstrates the fusion between Chinese cooking methods and Peruvian ingredients. Now a beloved staple of home cooking and restaurants across Peru, lomo saltado showcases the culinary mestizaje, or mixing, that characterizes much of Peruvian cuisine today.

Ingredients
  • sirloin steak
  • vegetable oil
  • red onion
  • tomato
  • garlic
  • soy sauce
  • white vinegar
  • cilantro
  • russet potatoes
  • aji amarillo paste
  • jasmine rice
Steps
  1. Heat 1 tablespoon of oil in a large pan over high heat. Add the steak, season with salt and pepper, and cook until browned, 5-6 minutes. Remove from the pan.
  2. Heat the remaining tablespoon of oil in the same pan, then add the red onion and cook for about 5 minutes, until softened and browned. Add the tomato, garlic, and aji amarillo paste, and cook for another 5-7 minutes, until the tomatoes have released some of their juices, but are still intact.
  3. Add the soy sauce and vinegar and stir to combine, let cook for 1 minute.
  4. Add the steak, fries, and cilantro. Toss gently to coat the fries in the sauce.
  5. Serve with rice, if desired
